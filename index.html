About

Flood hazard mapping is essential for disaster prevention but...

It remains challenging in data-scarce regions, where traditional hydrodynamic models require extensive geophysical inputs. We introduce ZeroFlood, a framework that leverages the geospatial foundation model: TerraMind to predict flood hazard maps. It relies only on SAR or multispectral imagery to identify flood-prone regions. This capability is enabled by a Thinking-in-Modality process, which compensates for missing spatial information such as land use/land cover (LULC) and digital elevation models (DEM). This demo page presents an interactive exploration of ZeroFlood, including: (1) visualizations of sample locations and corresponding model predictions, (2) a schematic overview of the model architecture and data flow, (3) descriptions of dataset creation and spatial distribution, and (4) experimental results comparing performance with baseline models.
